Project Description

Authors:
Gavin Goddard & Marius Tali
The ME 507 term project we pursued was a self-rolling ball that could change direction with center of mass manipulation. This was a replacement for competing in the hungry-hippo competition. The self-rolling ball consists of an acrylic orb that houses all our electronics and mechanical components. The ball has 3 directional movements in the x-y plane along with the ability to turn and drift. The turning was accomplished with a control law and the spinning of a brushed motor with encoder that was mounted to a disc with large rotational inertia. This design mimics a reaction wheel and allows for conservation of momentum to spin up or down the ball. To achieve a more interesting x-y directional movement on the ground, we implemented three omni wheels to allow for drifting and quick changes in rotation of the ball that would not be otherwise possible.
The ball was controlled with a wireless remote which communicated over Bluetooth to set motor duty cycles and operation modes. On this remote there was a 9-Axis IMU that would read heading, roll, and pitch data in the form of Euler angles and send that data to the robot, where it would be compared with the robotâ€™s own IMU data. Once compared, trigonometric calculations are performed, then duty cycles for each of the drive motors are set. In addition to this IMU control mode, we also have a button control mode and a camera control mode. The button control mode allows the user to press different buttons on the remote corresponding to each motor on the robot. Finally, the camera mode was not completed for this project, however, it was almost entirely integrated.
